"Creation, Not Certification"

Redesigning UTM's 3D Printing Service Around User Intent
Yuxiao (Tom) Chen | CCT477H5 | University of Toronto Mississauga

Contextual Inquiry

UTM Library Makerspace offers 3D printing—but its rigid, uniform certification workflow mismatches diverse student intentions and creates unnecessary friction.

-Methods & Setting-

• 12 hours of observation + brief interviews near Makerspace entrance (Oct 15-21, 2025)

• Ethical protocol: Verbal consent, anonymization, voluntary participation

• Focus: Remote print submission process via Gerstein portal

"Students want creation, not certification—UTM's system teaches before it empowers."

-Key Observations-

• Students repeatedly questioned: "Do I really need to watch this whole video again?"

• Experienced users complained about "being treated like a first-timer every time"

• Administrative burden (screenshots, forms) caused abandonment of print jobs

"When given agency in design decisions, students naturally gravitate toward systems that balance safety with autonomy."

Findings & Design Implications

-Methodological Context-

• n=7 complete responses (4 beginners, 3 experienced)

• Sessions averaged 8 minutes—critical during final academic weeks

• All participants completed electronic consent forms

• Limitation: Small sample size from personal networks; future work will expand recruitment

Transparency & efficiency are universal needs; bureaucracy is universally rejected regardless of expertise level.

Design Directions

From certification gate → creation gateway: Four evidence-based principles derived from participatory data

1

Intent-First Access

Landing page asks: "What do you want to do today?" → routes to Print / Learn / Expert flow. This addresses the core mismatch between institutional procedure and user purpose identified in contextual inquiry.

2

Tiered Safety

• Print&Go: 5-min video + waiver
• Learn&Create: + quiz (80% pass)
• Expert: self-certify + liability waiver

Data insight: Even beginners rejected comprehensive training in favor of targeted safety information.

3

Transparent Queue

Real-time dashboard: "Your print #3 in queue • Est. ready in 47 min" with completion notification. Universal need (7/7 participants) regardless of expertise level.

4

Progressive Trust

Auto-approval after 3 successful prints → unlocks direct access to printers. Rewards competence over compliance, addressing expert frustration with redundant oversight.
